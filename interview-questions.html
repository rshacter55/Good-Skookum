Sure, below is a customer discovery interview questionnaire using design thinking principles for your non-profit startup idea focused on addressing loneliness and isolation among individuals aged 50+.

Understanding the Customer:

  1. Can you tell me about yourself and your experiences with feelings of loneliness and isolation?
  2. In what ways do you currently seek social connection and support in your life?
  3. How has loneliness impacted your overall well-being and quality of life?
  4. What are some activities or hobbies that you enjoy and that help you feel connected to others?
  5. Can you share with me a specific instance when you felt particularly lonely or isolated?

Context and Objectives:

  1. How important is it for you to feel a sense of community and belonging at this stage of your life?
  2. What do you hope to achieve by being part of a supportive community focused on addressing loneliness?
  3. In what ways do you think being part of such a community could improve your day-to-day life?
  4. How do you envision your ideal support network and community to be like?

Identifying Problems and Solutions:

  1. What are the biggest challenges you face when trying to combat feelings of loneliness and isolation?
  2. Have you considered any other solutions or initiatives to address these challenges?
  3. How do you currently cope with feelings of loneliness when they arise?
  4. What do you think is missing in existing support systems for individuals struggling with loneliness and isolation?
  5. Can you provide examples of situations where you felt disconnected from those around you?

Assessing Value and Adoption:

  1. How valuable do you believe a platform for forming supportive communities to combat loneliness would be for you and others?
  2. What factors would make you hesitant to join or participate in a community-based initiative like the one proposed?
  3. In your opinion, what would be a reasonable cost or investment for access to such a supportive community?
  4. Can you share any past experiences of participating in community programs or support groups aimed at addressing social isolation?

Feel free to customize or add more questions based on your specific needs or insights you are looking to gather during the customer discovery phase.

When conducting customer interviews, seek truth about the customer's pain points, needs, and behaviors, not validation for your idea. Listen more than you speak and be prepared to pivot based on what you genuinely learn.
